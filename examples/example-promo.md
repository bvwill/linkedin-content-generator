```markdown
## Post

Every PM I talk to says the same thing about AI: "I know I should be using it more, but I don't know where to start."

Here's where I'd start: your next PRD.

Not because PRDs are the most important thing you do. But because they're familiar enough that you can see exactly what changes when AI is part of the process.

Take your last PRD. Feed it to Claude Code with this prompt: "Review this PRD. Identify gaps in the user stories, missing edge cases, and any assumptions that aren't validated."

In 2 minutes, you'll get feedback that usually takes a peer review cycle to surface. Not perfect feedback — but directional enough to make your next draft materially better.

That's the pattern: start with a workflow you already know, add AI to one step, and see what changes. Then expand from there.

This is exactly how we structured the AI-Native PM Bootcamp. Not "here's a list of AI tools." Instead: take your actual PM workflows and rebuild them with AI integrated at every step.

If you're a PM who wants to stop wondering about AI and start using it, the next cohort starts in March. Link in comments.

## Coaching Notes

- **Hook strength:** Strong — captures a universal feeling ("I know I should but I don't know where to start") that the target audience recognizes instantly
- **Pillar alignment:** Perfect bootcamp promo — teaches a real skill, then bridges to the offering
- **Engagement potential:** Moderate comments (people sharing their AI starting points), high link clicks if the CTA resonates
- **Alternative angles:** Could isolate just the PRD workflow as a pure tips post (no promo), or could do a storytelling version about a student's transformation
```

---

## Why This Works

1. **Opens with empathy, not pitch:** "Every PM I talk to says..." — Will is listening, not selling
2. **Teaches something real:** The Claude Code PRD review prompt is genuinely useful. A reader who never clicks the link still got value.
3. **Value is 60%+ of the post:** The first 10 lines are pure teaching. The bootcamp mention doesn't appear until line 12.
4. **Bridge is natural:** "This is exactly how we structured the bootcamp" — the transition from lesson to product feels earned, not forced
5. **CTA is low-pressure:** "Link in comments" — not "DM me NOW" or "Only 5 spots left!"
6. **No fabricated outcomes:** No fake testimonials or invented metrics. The value prop is experiential.
