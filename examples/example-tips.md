## Post

I used to spend 3 hours writing a competitive analysis. Now it takes 40 minutes.

Here's the exact Claude Code workflow I use:

**1. Seed the research**
I give Claude Code a single prompt: "Research the top 5 competitors in [space]. For each, find pricing, key features, recent funding, and primary customer segment."

It runs 10-15 web searches in parallel and pulls the data into a structured table in under 5 minutes.

**2. Add my analysis layer**
The raw data is a starting point, not the answer. I spend 20 minutes adding what AI can't: which competitors my stakeholders actually worry about, where our positioning is weakest, and what the data doesn't show about market dynamics.

**3. Format for the audience**
One more prompt to restructure the doc for my audience — exec summary for leadership, detailed comparison for the product team. Same data, two formats, 5 minutes.

The 3-hour version wasn't better. It was just slower.

AI doesn't replace your product judgment. It removes the busywork that sits between you and the thinking that matters.
